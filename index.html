Timothéé Chalamet

ABOUT

  • Born: December 27, 1995, in Hell's Kitchen, New York City, to a family deeply rooted in the arts and filmmaking.
  • Heritage: He holds dual American and French citizenship; he spent his summers in France as a child and is fully bilingual.
  • Education: A standout student at LaGuardia High School of Music & Art and Performing Arts, where he first realized his passion for professional acting.
  • Early Career: Before his big break, he appeared in the spy thriller series Homeland and had a small but memorable role in Christopher Nolan’s Interstellar.
  • Breakthrough: Became a global sensation with Call Me by Your Name (2017), earning an Oscar nomination and becoming the third-youngest nominee in that category.
  • Major Roles: He has starred in critically acclaimed films such as Lady Bird, Beautiful Boy, and Little Women, proving his ability to handle complex emotional depth.
  • Fashion Icon: Known for his unique, gender-fluid style, he frequently tops "Best Dressed" lists and was the first solo male to ever grace the cover of British Vogue.
  • Artistic Impact: Often described as the "definitive actor of his generation," he is praised by directors for his intense preparation and natural vulnerability on screen.
