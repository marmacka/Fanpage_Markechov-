DUNE

Directed by Denis Villeneuve, Dune (2021) and Dune: Part Two (2024) saw Timothée Chalamet take on the iconic role of Paul Atreides. Based on Frank Herbert’s classic novel, the story follows a brilliant young nobleman thrust into a deadly war for Arrakis, a desert planet containing the universe's most precious resource: "the spice."

PRODUCTION & DEDICATION

Chalamet’s performance was hailed for its growth from a hesitant boy to a powerful leader. To prepare, he spent months filming in the intense heat of the Jordan and Abu Dhabi deserts. He famously claimed that the "sand got everywhere," but the harsh environment helped him tap into Paul's isolation and the grueling nature of Arrakis.

AWARDS & GLOBAL IMPACT

The Dune franchise became a massive critical and commercial success, winning numerous Academy Awards. It transformed Chalamet into a global blockbuster leading man, proving he could command a massive budget film with the same emotional depth he brought to indie dramas. The films are celebrated for their stunning visuals and Hans Zimmer’s immersive score.
